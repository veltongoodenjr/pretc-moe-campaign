Anti-Bullying Campaign – Digital Strategy

#StandUpFiWiYouth

Submitted by Velton Gooden Jr for PR Etc.

Part 1: General Strategy

In Jamaica, nearly 60% of students have experienced bullying, and 30% avoid school because of it. This campaign, spearheaded by the Ministry of Education and Youth, seeks to raise awareness, mobilise engagement across stakeholders, and cultivate safe online and offline spaces.

Objectives: Drive national awareness, increase student participation, and promote bystander action, while equipping teachers and parents to respond effectively.

Target Audience: Primary – Jamaican students aged 11–18. Secondary – Parents, educators, school administrators.

Channels: Instagram, TikTok, Facebook, YouTube, and a dedicated campaign microsite on youthjamaica.gov.jm.

KPIs: Hashtag reach, video challenge entries, social engagement metrics, resource downloads, and increased help-seeking based on post-campaign surveys.

Part 2: Social Media Content Ideas

Instagram – “Your Story, Your Strength”

Type: Reels + Carousels

Student-led videos sharing how dem overcome bullying. Ends with captions like “Nuh worry yuhself, help deh bout.”

Visual Style: School settings, authentic talk, bold colours. “Likkle but tallawah” spirit throughout.

TikTok – #SpeakUpChallenge

Type: Short-form video

Students show creative ways to stop bullying or support a friend. Launched with Jamaican youth influencers.

Visual Style: Fast-paced, bold captions, music-driven. Inspired by local TikTok usage.

Facebook – Parent & Teacher Toolkit

Type: Infographics + Animations

Explains how to identify signs of bullying and respond supportively. Downloadable PDFs included.

Visual Style: Calming palette, easy-to-read fonts, family-friendly icons.

Part 3: Digital Marketing Amplifier

Initiative: #OneLoveOneHeart Challenge Week

A high-energy campaign week where schools post videos showing unity against bullying. Skits, chants or dances are welcomed, all tied to the theme: “One love, one heart – one voice against bullying.”

The challenge launches with well-known influencers Daindra Harrison and Juliet Angelique Bodley (Julie Mango), using their signature humour to start each skit light and relatable — but mid-way, the energy shifts to show a serious, real-world bullying moment.

Each video ends with a formal sit-down on camera, where the influencer drops character to speak honestly: “Jokes aside, bullying serious. We affi talk 'bout it.” This blend of entertainment and authenticity makes the message stick.

Participating schools can win branded gear or wellness workshops. The challenge taps into the culture of dance, storytelling and humour — but grounds it in real-life experiences, making it impactful and memorable for students islandwide.
